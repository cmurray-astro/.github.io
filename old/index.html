Claire E. Murray

Astronomer

Welcome! I am a postdoctoral researcher in the ISM group at the Space Telescope Science Institute (STScI, aka "the Tute") in Baltimore, MD. I completed my PhD in the Department of Astronomy at the University of Wisconsin-Madison in August 2017. Before that, I attended Carleton College in Northfield, MN where I received B.A. in Physics in June 2011.

I spend my time thinking about how star-forming material cycles through the local interstellar medium (ISM) in various ionized, dusty, neutral and molecular phases. I am particularly interested in decoding the structure of the ISM and the nature of gas flows in galaxies.

That's me pictured, suspended 130 meters above the Arecibo Observatory's 305-meter dish!
